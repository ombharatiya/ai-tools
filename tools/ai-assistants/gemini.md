## What it is

Gemini is Google's general assistant for chat, research, coding, and image and video generation, tied into Google products like Search, Chrome, Android, and Workspace. It runs on Google DeepMind models, with Gemini 3.1 Pro as the flagship reasoning model (1M-token context) and Gemini 3.5 Flash as the faster default. Subscriptions are sold as Google AI tiers that also bundle Google One storage and YouTube perks.

## Best for

- Heavy Google and Android users who want AI inside Search, Chrome, Gmail, and Docs.
- People who already pay for Google One storage or YouTube Premium and can absorb the AI tier into that bundle.
- Users who want built-in image and video generation.

## Not for

- Privacy sensitive users uncomfortable with human review of chats and multi-year retention.
- People who want a simple single-purpose assistant without storage and YouTube bundling.
- Users outside the US who may not get US-only features.

## Strengths

- The deepest integration with products people already use: Search, Chrome, Android, Gmail, Docs, and the rest of Workspace.
- Strong multimodal range, including image and video generation and a 1M-token context on the flagship model.
- Subscriptions bundle real non-AI value: large Google One storage (up to 20 TB) and YouTube Premium.
- Aggressive 2026 pricing, with the Ultra entry cut to about $100 and a cheap roughly $8 Plus tier.
- Backed by Google DeepMind and Google's global infrastructure.

## Weaknesses and dealbreakers

- **Dealbreaker for sensitive use:** by default a subset of chats are reviewed by humans and used to improve models, and reviewed chats can be retained up to 3 years. Default activity retention is 18 months.
- The privacy opt-out has a cost: turning off Gemini Apps Activity (which stops human review and training) also disables long-term chat history, so you trade memory for privacy.
- Heavy bundling complexity: tiers mix AI usage, storage, and YouTube perks, and features differ by country and platform.
- Rollouts are staggered and region-gated. Some features are US or English only.
- Several models are in preview or rolling out rather than fully general, so advertised capabilities may not be live for everyone.

## Stability and maturity

Backed by Google and Google DeepMind with very large infrastructure and mature consumer distribution through Search, Android, and Workspace. Like its rivals, it changes fast and had incidents in the early-May 2026 cross-industry window. Bundling and per-region feature gating add complexity.

## Privacy and data

By default, a subset of Gemini app chats are reviewed by humans and used to improve Google's models. Reviewed chats can be kept up to 3 years, and default activity auto-deletes at 18 months (changeable to 3 or 36 months, or off). Turning off Gemini Apps Activity stops human review and training going forward but also removes long-term history. Google explicitly warns not to enter confidential information. Workspace and Cloud enterprise data is governed by separate, stricter terms.

## Integrations

Web, Android, iOS, Gemini in Chrome, and deep Workspace integration across Gmail, Docs, and more. Developer access via the Gemini API (Google AI Studio) and Vertex AI, including a Managed Agents API.

## Alternatives

- **[ChatGPT](chatgpt.md):** when you want the broadest standalone feature set and the largest ecosystem.
- **[Claude](claude.md):** when you want stronger coding and a no-training-by-default privacy posture.

## Bottom line

**Solid choice**, and the natural pick if you already live in Google products and want AI inside Search, Gmail, and Docs, with bundled storage and YouTube sweetening the deal. Capability is strong, especially multimodal. The reason it is not a top pick for everyone is privacy: the default includes human review of a subset of chats and multi-year retention, and the opt-out costs you your chat history. For Google-centric users that tradeoff may be fine. For privacy-first users, Claude is the safer default.
