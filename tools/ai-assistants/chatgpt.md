## What it is

ChatGPT is a general assistant you talk to in plain language to write, analyze, code, search the web, generate images, and use voice. It runs on OpenAI's GPT-5.5 series and is the most widely used consumer AI product. It is available on web, mobile, desktop apps, and through a mature developer API.

## Best for

- General consumers and professionals who want the most capable, feature-rich all-rounder.
- People who want voice and image generation in one app.
- Developers who want the largest API ecosystem.

## Not for

- Privacy sensitive users who do not want training-on-by-default or ads on cheaper tiers.
- People who want a stable, slow-changing product without frequent model renames.

## Pricing

Free tier with tight limits. Go is $8/mo, Plus is $20/mo, with higher Pro tiers and usage based API billing. Free and Go are ad-supported for US adult users. Last verified 2026-06-05.

## Strengths

- The largest user base and ecosystem, so the most tutorials, integrations, and community knowledge.
- Strong all-rounder across writing, coding, reasoning, image generation, and voice in one product.
- Broad feature set on paid tiers: Advanced Voice, image generation, Canvas, Deep Research, a coding agent, and a large connector catalog.
- Wide platform coverage: web, iOS, Android, native Windows and macOS apps, and a mature API.
- The lowest paid entry points among the big three, with Go at $8 and a usable free tier.

## Weaknesses and dealbreakers

- **Dealbreaker for privacy sensitive users:** it trains on your conversations by default on Free, Plus, and Pro personal accounts. You must manually opt out, and short-term abuse-monitoring retention still applies.
- Ads now appear on Free and Go for US adults since 2026-02-09. To remove them you upgrade to Plus.
- Free limits are tight (reported around 10 messages per 5 hours), so heavy free use is impractical.
- Confusing tier sprawl: Free, Go, two Pro prices, Business, Enterprise, plus credits.
- Notable multi-service outages in 2026 affected app, login, voice, and API together.

## Stability and maturity

Backed by OpenAI with deep Microsoft investment and large infrastructure. Uptime across core components was reported around 99.83% over a recent 90-day window, though there were several visible outages in 2026. Features and model names change very frequently, sometimes month to month.

## Privacy and data

On by default: OpenAI may train on conversations from Free, Plus, and Pro personal accounts. You opt out under Settings, Data Controls, "Improve the model for everyone." Temporary Chat is excluded from training and history. Business, Enterprise, and API data is not used for training by default, and Enterprise adds SOC 2 Type II, SSO, and admin controls. Opting out does not stop short-term retention for abuse monitoring.

## Integrations

Web, iOS, Android, native Windows and macOS desktop apps, and the OpenAI API. Paid tiers include many app connectors such as Slack, Google Drive, and Salesforce. A coding agent integrates with IDEs and can be triggered from mobile.

## Alternatives

- **[Claude](claude.md):** when you want stronger coding, long-document work, and a no-training-by-default privacy posture.
- **[Gemini](gemini.md):** when you live in Google products and want AI inside Search, Gmail, and Docs.

## Bottom line

**Top pick** for the broadest, most capable all-rounder, especially if you want voice and image generation in one place and the largest ecosystem. The honest caveat is privacy: it trains on personal-tier chats by default and shows ads on its cheaper tiers in the US. Turn off training in settings, and if privacy is your main concern, Claude has a better default. For raw breadth of features, ChatGPT is hard to beat.
