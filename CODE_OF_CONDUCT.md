# Code of Conduct

## The short version

Be respectful. Assume good faith. Keep it about the tools, not the people.

## What we expect

- Treat others with courtesy, including when you disagree.
- Critique tools and reviews, not the contributors behind them.
- Welcome newcomers and help them meet the standards.
- Disclose conflicts of interest honestly.
- Accept correction gracefully when you get a fact wrong.

## What is not allowed

- Harassment, insults, or personal attacks.
- Discrimination or demeaning comments based on who someone is.
- Posting private information without consent.
- Vendor astroturfing: pretending to be a neutral contributor while promoting a tool you are tied to.
- Spam, including affiliate links and undisclosed promotion.

## Scope

This applies in all project spaces: issues, pull requests, discussions, and any related channels.

## Reporting

If you see behavior that breaks these rules, open an issue or contact a maintainer. Reports are handled discreetly. Maintainers may edit, remove, or reject contributions and may block repeat offenders.

## Spirit

This project works because people trust it. That trust extends to how we treat each other. When in doubt, be kind and be honest.
