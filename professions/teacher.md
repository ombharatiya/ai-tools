# AI tools and apps for a Teacher and Educator

For K-12 and higher-ed teachers who plan, teach, assess, and communicate. AI can save real time on planning and feedback. A 2025 Gallup and Walton survey found about 60% of US K-12 teachers used an AI tool for work that year, and weekly users estimated saving about six hours a week. The catch is student-data privacy, which you must handle carefully.

## Who this is for

Classroom teachers, special education teachers, and college instructors, plus the instructional staff who support them.

## Starter stack

1. **MagicSchool AI** (freemium, review pending): the broadest set of planning, feedback, and IEP tools.
2. **Khanmigo** (freemium, review pending): free for US teachers, nonprofit-built, with a student tutor.
3. **Canva for Education** (free, review pending): free for verified teachers, handles visuals and slides.
4. **NotebookLM** (freemium, review pending): source-grounded summarizing and study guides.
5. **Brisk Teaching** (freemium, review pending): if you live in Google Docs and Classroom.

## Tasks and the tools that help

### Lesson planning, worksheets, and differentiation

- **MagicSchool AI** (freemium, review pending): dozens of teacher tools for plans, worksheets, and rubrics.
- **Brisk Teaching** (freemium, review pending): a Chrome extension that works inside Google Docs, Slides, and Classroom.
- **Diffit** (freemium, review pending): reading passages at different grade levels, with questions and vocabulary.

### Grading and written feedback

- **Gradescope** (paid, review pending): rubric-based grading of problem sets and exams, including handwritten work.
- **MagicSchool** or **Brisk** (freemium, review pending): AI first-draft feedback on student writing.
- [ChatGPT](../tools/ai-assistants/chatgpt.md), [Claude](../tools/ai-assistants/claude.md), or [Gemini](../tools/ai-assistants/gemini.md): can draft feedback, but only on anonymized work (see cautions).

### Formative assessment and practice

- **Khanmigo** (freemium, review pending): an AI tutor plus teacher tools, free for US teachers.
- **Curipod** (freemium, review pending): interactive slide lessons with polls and AI feedback.
- **Quizizz** or **Quizlet** (freemium, review pending): AI-assisted quiz and practice generation.

### Parent and student communication

- **MagicSchool** or **Brisk** (freemium, review pending): draft emails, newsletters, and notes, with translation into many languages.
- [Gemini](../tools/ai-assistants/gemini.md) in Workspace, Copilot in Microsoft 365, or [ChatGPT](../tools/ai-assistants/chatgpt.md): draft and refine routine communications.

### Slides and visual materials

- **Canva for Education** (free, review pending): slides, worksheets, and images, free for verified teachers.
- **Gamma** (freemium, review pending): full slide decks from a prompt or outline.

### Special education and accessibility

- **MagicSchool IEP tools** (freemium, review pending): draft IEP goals and accommodations for your review. A common reason special-ed teachers pay.
- **Speechify** (freemium, review pending): text to speech for accessibility.

## Cautions for this profession

- **Student data privacy is the big one.** The most common FERPA problem is pasting student names, grades, or writing into general AI tools that lack a school agreement. Use district-approved tools with a signed Data Privacy Agreement, and de-identify work before using any consumer tool.
- **COPPA changed.** New rules took effect in 2025 with full compliance required by April 2026, shifting toward opt-in consent for data on children under 13. A 2024 survey found many districts using AI had not signed data agreements with vendors. Verify your tool and your district policy.
- **AI detectors are not proof.** Independent testing shows detection drops on edited text and false positives fall hardest on non-native English speakers. Several universities disabled AI detection in 2025 and 2026, and Turnitin says its score should not be the sole basis for an accusation. Do not treat a detector score as evidence of cheating.
- **Hallucination.** AI-generated facts, citations, passages, and answer keys can be wrong. Review before putting anything in front of students. Source-grounded tools (NotebookLM) reduce but do not remove this.
- **Vendor compliance claims are self-reported.** "FERPA aligned" on a marketing page is not a signed agreement reviewed by your district. Verify.

## How to choose

Start with one broad teacher platform (MagicSchool) and one source-grounded research tool (NotebookLM), and use whatever general assistant your district already approves. Add Khanmigo for student-facing tutoring. Keep every AI output under your own review before it reaches a student or a parent.
