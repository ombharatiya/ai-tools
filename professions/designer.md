# AI tools and apps for a Designer

For graphic and UX/UI designers: logos, layouts, and marketing graphics through to app interfaces, wireframes, and prototypes. AI speeds up concepting and production, but the legal picture for generated art is unsettled, so read the cautions.

## Who this is for

Graphic designers, brand and marketing designers, and UX/UI designers and researchers.

## Starter stack

1. **Figma** (freemium, review pending): the core UI/UX tool. Learn its AI features in place.
2. **Canva** (freemium, review pending): fast branded graphics and easy image edits.
3. **Adobe Firefly** (freemium, review pending): generative images with a commercial-safety story.
4. [Midjourney](../tools/image-generation/midjourney.md) (paid): the strongest concept imagery, with the IP cautions below.

## Tasks and the tools that help

### Generating images, concepts, and style exploration

- [Midjourney](../tools/image-generation/midjourney.md) (paid): high-quality, stylized image generation for early concepting.
- **Adobe Firefly** (freemium, review pending): image, vector, and video generation trained on licensed content, marketed as commercially safer.
- [OpenAI image generation](../tools/image-generation/dall-e.md) (freemium): strong prompt following and text in images.
- [Stable Diffusion](../tools/image-generation/stable-diffusion.md) (open source): run locally for full control and privacy.

### Editing and retouching images

- **Adobe Photoshop (Generative Fill)** (paid, review pending): generative fill, expand, and object removal in the pro editor.
- **Canva Magic Studio** (freemium, review pending): easy edits, background removal, and expand for non-experts.
- **Topaz** (paid, review pending): AI upscaling and sharpening.

### Layout, social graphics, and on-brand assets

- **Canva Magic Studio** (freemium, review pending): text to design, brand kit, and bulk content.
- **Adobe Express** (freemium, review pending): quick branded graphics with Firefly features.

### UI design, wireframes, and first-draft screens

- **Figma (Figma Make and AI)** (freemium, review pending): first-draft generation using your own design-system components.
- **UX Pilot** (freemium, review pending): generates research questions, low-fidelity wireframes, and usability feedback.
- **Uizard** (freemium, review pending): turns prompts and sketches into mockups. Now part of Figma.

### User research synthesis (UX)

- **Dovetail** (freemium, review pending): stores research and uses AI to transcribe, tag, and find themes.
- **Maze** (freemium, review pending): usability testing with AI summaries and friction analysis.

### Design to code and handoff

- **Figma Make** (freemium, review pending): moves from design toward working front end inside Figma.
- **v0 by Vercel** (freemium, review pending): turns prompts or designs into front-end code.
- [Cursor](../tools/coding-assistants/cursor.md) (freemium): for designers who code, turn designs into components.

## Cautions for this profession

- **Copyright lawsuits are unsettled.** Image generators are in active litigation over training data and outputs (Getty v Stability AI, and Disney, Universal, and Warner Bros. Discovery suits against Midjourney over outputs resembling their characters). Treat outputs that resemble known characters, logos, or living artists' styles as legal risk, especially for commercial work.
- **You may not own AI-generated art.** In the US, purely AI-generated images are not copyrightable. For work a client needs to own and defend, this matters. Human editing and arrangement can add protectable authorship.
- **Commercial-use safety varies.** Firefly markets itself as safer because of its licensed training set. Many generators put the IP risk on you. Read each tool's commercial-use and indemnification terms before client delivery.
- **Trademarks and likenesses.** Generating brand logos, celebrity faces, or recognizable IP can infringe trademark or publicity rights regardless of copyright.
- **Privacy in UX research.** Interview recordings hold personal data. Check storage, retention, and AI-training terms against your consent agreements and any GDPR or CCPA duties. Anonymize where you can.
- **Bias and sameness.** Generators can produce stereotyped or generic results and can miss accessibility (contrast, hierarchy, alt text). Keep human judgment and accessibility checks in the loop.

## How to choose

For UI and UX, Figma is the hub and its AI features are worth learning first. For graphics and social, Canva covers most needs cheaply. For generated imagery, weigh look against legal safety: Firefly leans safer for commercial work, Midjourney leans toward the best look, and Stable Diffusion gives you local control.
