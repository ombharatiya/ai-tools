# AI tools and apps for a Customer Support Agent

For people who resolve customer questions across chat, email, phone, and social. AI can deflect routine tickets and draft replies, but a wrong answer about a refund or policy is a real harm, so grounding and human handoff matter.

## Who this is for

Frontline support agents, support team leads, and small teams choosing their first AI support setup.

## Starter stack

1. Your **helpdesk's built-in AI** (Zendesk AI, Intercom Fin, or Freshdesk Freddy): the fastest path, since it sits in the tool you already use.
2. [ChatGPT](../tools/ai-assistants/chatgpt.md) or [Claude](../tools/ai-assistants/claude.md) (freemium): rewriting, summarizing, and tone fixes.
3. **NotebookLM** (free, review pending): turn internal docs into clear answers and draft articles.
4. **DeepL** (freemium, review pending): quick, good-quality translation.
5. **Chatwoot** (open source, review pending): if you want to self-host on a budget.

## Tasks and the tools that help

### Automated first-line resolution (AI agents)

- **Intercom Fin** (paid, review pending): an AI agent that resolves tickets and can take actions, usage-based pricing.
- **Zendesk AI** (paid, review pending): AI resolution native to Zendesk.
- **Ada** (enterprise, review pending): an AI resolution platform, quote-driven.
- **Gorgias** (paid, review pending): ecommerce-focused support with an AI agent, strong with Shopify.

### Agent assist (suggested replies, knowledge surfacing)

- **Zendesk**, **Intercom**, or **Freshdesk (Freddy)** (paid, review pending): in-workflow suggestions and drafted replies.
- **Help Scout** (paid, review pending): support platform with AI answers and drafting.

### Writing and rewriting responses

- [ChatGPT](../tools/ai-assistants/chatgpt.md) or [Claude](../tools/ai-assistants/claude.md) (freemium): rephrasing, summarizing, and softening tone.
- Built-in helpdesk AI: expand, rephrase, and change-tone features inside the agent console.

### Knowledge base creation and upkeep

- Helpdesk-native KB AI (Zendesk, Intercom): drafts and updates articles from tickets and docs.
- **NotebookLM** (free, review pending): synthesizes internal docs into draft articles grounded in your sources.

### Multilingual support and translation

- **Freshdesk (Freddy)** (paid, review pending): native support in many languages with real-time translation.
- **DeepL** (freemium, review pending): high-quality machine translation for drafting replies.

### Quality assurance and coaching

- **Klaus (Zendesk QA)** (paid, review pending): AI scoring across a large share of conversations.
- **MaestroQA** (enterprise, review pending): dedicated QA with scorecards and calibration.

## Cautions for this profession

- **Hallucination is the central risk.** An AI agent that invents a policy, refund amount, or instruction creates real harm and liability. Ground AI in your verified knowledge base, and keep humans in the loop for anything sensitive (refunds, account changes, legal, medical, financial).
- **Customer PII and compliance.** Support conversations hold personal and sometimes regulated data. Confirm the tool's data handling, retention, sub-processors, and certifications (SOC 2, GDPR, HIPAA where relevant). Do not paste customer PII into consumer AI tools.
- **Escalation and handoff.** Make sure the AI cleanly hands off to a human when unsure or when asked. Poor handoff is a top complaint.
- **Tone and brand safety.** AI can sound cold, over-apologize, or promise things you cannot keep. Review templates and set guardrails on what it may say.
- **Translation accuracy.** Machine translation can miss nuance and be confidently wrong. Have a fluent human review sensitive or legal messages.
- **Confusing pricing.** Vendors charge per resolution, per interaction, per agent, or per session, and some double bill. Model your real volume before committing.
- **Over-automation backfires.** Deflecting too hard frustrates customers. Measure genuine resolution and satisfaction, not just deflection rate.

## How to choose

If you already have a helpdesk, start with its built-in AI rather than bolting on a separate tool. Choose a usage-based agent (Intercom Fin, Ada) when volume is high and answers are well documented. If cost is the constraint and you have technical help, Chatwoot plus a general assistant is a workable open-source base.
