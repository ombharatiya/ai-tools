# AI tools and apps for an Accountant and Bookkeeper

For people who record, reconcile, and report financial information, and who may prepare taxes, audit, or advise. AI is genuinely good at categorization, data capture, and first-draft analysis. You stay responsible for accuracy, confidentiality, and what gets filed.

> **Caution: this is a regulated field.** Client financial and taxpayer data must stay out of tools that train on inputs or share data without authorization. Verify AI conclusions against primary authority (the Internal Revenue Code, regulations, and rulings), not the AI's summary. Treat every output as a draft a qualified human must review and sign.

## Who this is for

Bookkeepers, accountants, CPAs, and tax preparers, in firms or running their own practice.

## Starter stack

With the caution that professional judgment, review of every output, and confirmation against primary authority are required:

1. A core ledger with built-in AI: **QuickBooks Online (Intuit Assist)** or **Xero**. Review every auto-categorization and reconciliation.
2. A document-capture tool: **Dext** or **Hubdoc**, to cut manual entry. Spot-check extractions.
3. For accounts-payable-heavy work: **Bill (Bill.com)** for small and mid clients, or **Vic.ai** at larger scale.
4. A tax research tool if you do tax: **Checkpoint Edge with CoCounsel**, **CCH AnswerConnect**, or **Blue J**. Verify answers against primary authority.
5. A general assistant ([ChatGPT](../tools/ai-assistants/chatgpt.md), [Claude](../tools/ai-assistants/claude.md), or [Gemini](../tools/ai-assistants/gemini.md)) on a paid no-training tier, for de-identified analysis and client-friendly drafting only.

- **Dext** (paid, review pending): established receipt, invoice, and statement extraction for firms.
- **AutoEntry** (paid, review pending): data capture into accounting systems.

- **Vic.ai** (enterprise, review pending): autonomous AP automation for mid-market and enterprise.
- **Bill (Bill.com)** (paid, review pending): AP and AR automation widely used by small businesses and firms.

- **CCH AnswerConnect** (enterprise, review pending): tax research with AI and multi-state charts.

## Cautions for this profession

- **Client confidentiality and data protection.** CPAs are bound by the AICPA Confidential Client Information Rule, and tax preparers by IRC Section 7216, which restricts use or disclosure of return information and generally requires client consent. Use tools with clear no-training terms and a data agreement, and get informed consent where required.
- **Disclosure of AI use.** AICPA guidance addresses whether and how to disclose generative-AI use to clients. Decide your policy deliberately, with counsel if needed.
- **Accuracy and due care.** AI can miscategorize transactions, misread documents, invent tax authority, or produce confident but wrong analysis. Vendor accuracy claims are conditional. A human must review output, and for tax and audit work you must confirm conclusions against primary authority.
- **Audit independence and governance.** For audit and assurance work, COSO's 2026 guidance and AICPA guidance push firms to keep an audit trail of prompts and outputs, assess tool risk, and maintain controls. AI does not reduce the auditor's responsibility for evidence and skepticism.
- **Professional liability.** You remain responsible for filings and advice. Over-reliance without review can create liability, including penalties tied to inaccurate returns.
- **Privacy and security.** Financial data is sensitive and often regulated (for example GLBA, plus state privacy laws). Confirm encryption, access controls, SOC 2, and where data is stored.

## How to choose

Start with the ledger and document-capture tools, since categorization and data entry are where AI saves the most time, and review the output. Add AP automation as invoice volume grows, and a dedicated tax research tool only if you do tax work, always checking against primary authority. Keep client data out of consumer chatbot tiers.
