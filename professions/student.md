# AI tools and apps for a Student

For high school and university students who learn, research, write, and prepare for exams. AI is a strong tutor and editor. Used wrong, it is plagiarism and it hollows out the skills you are paying to build, so use it to learn, not to ghostwrite.

## Who this is for

High school and college students across subjects, including those taking quantitative or coding courses.

## Starter stack

1. **NotebookLM** (freemium, review pending): grounded research and study guides from your own sources.
2. One general assistant: [ChatGPT](../tools/ai-assistants/chatgpt.md), [Claude](../tools/ai-assistants/claude.md), or [Gemini](../tools/ai-assistants/gemini.md) (freemium) for tutoring and drafting.
3. **Grammarly** or **QuillBot** (freemium, review pending): proofreading and paraphrasing.
4. **Otter.ai** (freemium, review pending): lecture transcription.
5. **Khanmigo** (freemium, review pending) or **Quizlet**: guided tutoring and flashcards.

## Tasks and the tools that help

### Understanding material and tutoring

- [ChatGPT](../tools/ai-assistants/chatgpt.md), [Claude](../tools/ai-assistants/claude.md), or [Gemini](../tools/ai-assistants/gemini.md) (freemium): explain concepts and work through problems step by step.
- **Khanmigo** (freemium, review pending): a Socratic tutor that nudges instead of handing over answers.
- **Wolfram Alpha** (freemium, review pending): reliable computational and step-by-step math and science.

### Research and literature review

- **NotebookLM** (freemium, review pending): answers grounded only in your uploaded sources, with citations back to the document.
- **Perplexity** (freemium, review pending): sourced answers with clickable references.
- **Elicit** (freemium, review pending) or **Consensus** (freemium, review pending): summarize findings across academic papers.

### Writing, editing, and proofreading

- **Grammarly** (freemium, review pending): grammar, clarity, and tone, inline in your docs.
- **QuillBot** (freemium, review pending): paraphrasing, summarizing, and citation help.
- [ChatGPT](../tools/ai-assistants/chatgpt.md) or [Claude](../tools/ai-assistants/claude.md): brainstorming, outlining, and revising your own drafts.

### Note-taking and lecture capture

- **Otter.ai** (freemium, review pending): records and transcribes lectures, with summaries.
- **Notion AI** (freemium, review pending): notes, tasks, and study materials in one workspace.

### Exam prep and active recall

- **Quizlet** (freemium, review pending): flashcards with AI study modes.
- **NotebookLM**, [ChatGPT](../tools/ai-assistants/chatgpt.md), or [Claude](../tools/ai-assistants/claude.md): generate practice questions from your own notes.

### Coding and quantitative coursework

- [GitHub Copilot](../tools/coding-assistants/github-copilot.md) (freemium): code completion and explanation, free for verified students through the Student Developer Pack.
- [ChatGPT](../tools/ai-assistants/chatgpt.md) or [Claude](../tools/ai-assistants/claude.md): debug, explain, and learn programming concepts.

## Cautions for this profession

- **Academic integrity is the central risk.** Submitting AI-written work as your own is misconduct at most schools and can mean failing or worse. Rules vary by course, so check the syllabus and ask if unsure. Use AI to learn, draft, and check, not to ghostwrite.
- **AI detectors are unreliable, both ways.** They produce false positives, especially against non-native English writers, so even your honest writing can be flagged. Keep drafts and version history to show your process.
- **Hallucination.** General chatbots invent facts, fake citations, and wrong math, sometimes confidently. Verify every fact and never cite a source the AI gave you without confirming it exists.
- **Privacy and cost.** Do not paste others' private data or unpublished work into consumer AI. Most students can do almost everything on free tiers plus one paid tool. "Free for students" offers are real but time-limited, so check before relying on them.
- **Learning loss.** Over-reliance hollows out the skills you are there to build. Treat AI as a tutor and editor, not a replacement for thinking.

## How to choose

Lead with source-grounded tools for research (NotebookLM, Consensus, Perplexity with links), since they hallucinate less. Add one general assistant for tutoring and one writing aid. You rarely need more than one paid subscription. Always keep your own process visible in case your work is questioned.
