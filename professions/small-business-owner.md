# AI tools and apps for a Small Business Owner

For founders and owners who do marketing, sales, support, content, operations, and finance, often solo or with a tiny team. AI can cover a lot of that ground cheaply. The trap is cost creep and publishing AI output without a human check.

## Who this is for

Solo founders, freelancers running a business, and owners of small teams who wear many hats.

## Starter stack

1. One general assistant: [ChatGPT](../tools/ai-assistants/chatgpt.md), [Claude](../tools/ai-assistants/claude.md), or [Gemini](../tools/ai-assistants/gemini.md) (freemium) for writing, planning, and support drafts.
2. **Canva** (freemium, review pending): all your visual and social content.
3. **Buffer** (freemium, review pending): social scheduling with AI captions.
4. A free-tier CRM: **HubSpot** or **Zoho** (freemium, review pending) to organize leads and email.
5. **QuickBooks** or **Xero** (paid, review pending): once finances outgrow a spreadsheet.

## Tasks and the tools that help

### Marketing copy and content

- [ChatGPT](../tools/ai-assistants/chatgpt.md), [Claude](../tools/ai-assistants/claude.md), or [Gemini](../tools/ai-assistants/gemini.md) (freemium): blog posts, product descriptions, ads, and email drafts. Often enough on its own.
- **Jasper** (paid, review pending) or **Copy.ai** (freemium, review pending): marketing-focused content with brand voice.

### Design, branding, and visuals

- **Canva** (freemium, review pending): logos, social graphics, flyers, and simple video.
- **Gamma** (freemium, review pending): pitch decks and proposals from a prompt.
- For generated imagery: [Midjourney](../tools/image-generation/midjourney.md) or [Stable Diffusion](../tools/image-generation/stable-diffusion.md).

### Social media management

- **Buffer** (freemium, review pending): scheduling with AI captions, good value for small teams.
- **Metricool** or **Publer** (freemium, review pending): budget-friendly scheduling with free tiers.

### Customer support

- **Tidio** (freemium, review pending): live chat plus an AI agent sized for small-business volume.
- A general assistant plus your FAQ: a cheap stopgap for drafting replies.

### Email, CRM, and lead follow-up

- **HubSpot (Breeze AI)** (freemium, review pending): a free CRM tier with AI to draft emails and summarize records.
- **Zoho CRM (Zia)** (freemium, review pending): an affordable CRM with an AI assistant.
- **Mailchimp** (freemium, review pending): email marketing with AI content and segmentation.

### Workflow automation

- **Zapier** (freemium, review pending): connect apps and automate tasks, now with AI building.
- **Make** (freemium, review pending): visual automation, often cheaper at scale.
- **n8n** (open source, review pending): self-host for free, good for cost-sensitive technical owners.

### Bookkeeping and finance

- **QuickBooks Online (Intuit Assist)** (paid, review pending): invoicing, categorization, and reconciliation with AI.
- **Xero** (paid, review pending): a QuickBooks alternative with AI features.
- **Booke AI** (paid, review pending): an AI bookkeeping layer on top of QuickBooks or Xero.

## Cautions for this profession

- **Cost creep is the practical trap.** Small businesses often run several AI tools at once, and $20 here and $30 there adds up. Start on free tiers, pay only when a tool clearly earns it, and audit subscriptions quarterly. Watch per-seat and per-usage pricing that scales with volume.
- **Accuracy and liability.** Do not publish AI output, especially in finance, legal, health, or regulated claims, without human review. AI invents facts and citations. For bookkeeping, AI categorization needs a human or accountant check before filing taxes.
- **Brand and legal risk.** AI can produce generic, off-brand, or unintentionally copied content. Check facts and tone, and review each tool's terms on commercial use and ownership. Disclose AI use where required.
- **Customer data and privacy.** Feeding customer personal data into consumer AI tools can breach privacy laws and your own policy. Use business tiers with proper data terms.
- **Lock-in and continuity.** Hosted tools change prices and features often. Keep exports of your data and avoid building a critical process around one tool you cannot replace. Open-source options like n8n reduce this risk.
- **Security.** Connecting many apps widens your attack surface. Use strong auth, limit permissions, and watch which tools can touch payments and customer systems.

## How to choose

You can run a whole small business on one general assistant, Canva, a free CRM, and a scheduler, all on free or low tiers. Add finance tools when a spreadsheet stops coping, and a dedicated support tool when ticket volume justifies it. Review your stack every quarter and cut what you are not using.
